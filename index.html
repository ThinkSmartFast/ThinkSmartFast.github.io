About

Hi, I am Iuliia Chervonooka.

MA student in Digital Humanities and English Language & Linguistics at the University of Vienna. My interests lie at the intersection of research, digital humanities, and user experience design, particularly in the areas of data visualization and digital knowledge environments.

My goal is to transform complex data and research insights into intuitive digital experiences that support exploration, understanding, and discovery.

With a professional background in digital communications and platform development, I am interested in designing interfaces that make data, research findings, and narratives accessible and engaging.

Projects

LexVAD

The central objective of LexVAD (Lexical Variation of the Austrian Dialects in the 20th century) is to make historical dialect data from the 20th century accessible and carry out geolinguistic mapping and analytical investigation of these materials.

Within this context, my primary task was conducting a structured requirements analysis to adapt the existing mapping tool developed for the LexAT21 project to the specific needs of LexVAD20.

My work combined research, design, and initial technical implementation. The main areas of responsibility included

  • Conducting a structured requirements analysis
  • Documenting findings and proposing improvements
  • Designing interface solutions and creating a prototype in Figma
  • Exploring implementation possibilities in Vue/Nuxt.js
